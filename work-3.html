Launch project

The Problem Opportunity

Each cohort, I observed the remarkable transformation in UX students. What I also observed, was what happened when the separate disciplines came together for the 3D hackathon (Data, Design, Development).

Each discipline had been heads down in their specialization for 10 weeks. While this was necessary to hone their skills, it meant there were unknowns : who were the other students, what were they learning, what was UX, and ultimately, how would they work together.

The impact during the course was the 3D Hackathon, where student feedback indicated challenges in collaboration and communication. The ultimate impact I saw was post program, where students could be empowered to break out of the pattern of silos in tech. This led me to wonder...

How might we make the unknown known
How might we make collaboration a core competency
How might we empower students to breakdown barriers
How might we teach a common language

The solution

Cross-discipline touch-points.

For Winter 2020, I designed and led BrainStations first cross-discipline lecture, including a hand-off lab between Design and Development. Future iterations might explore Design Systems as well as Storybook.

In partnership with the experience team, we also introduced 3 touch-points to build the foundations of communication and collaboration.

Meet the different disciplines
Explore overlap and what collaboration might look like
Practice workflow and handoff
